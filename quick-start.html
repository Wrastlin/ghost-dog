Key Findings

What we learned from reading 112 documents so you don't have to read all of them.

How TPA-Contractor Relationships Actually Work

  • TPAs don't pay contractors. The policyholder is the sole party obligated to pay. The TPA just manages the network and dispatches work orders.
  • TPAs charge 7-11% network fees on every job (Alacrity: 7% GC, 11% service-only, 5.5% tree removal).
  • Work orders are at TPA's sole discretion. No guaranteed volume. They can stop sending work at any time.
  • 30-day termination by either party is standard (Alacrity).
  • Contractors must indemnify the TPA against all claims and judgments. No lost profits or consequential damages recoverable.
  • Compensation = Xactimate unit prices modified by an "Adjustment Factor" set by the TPA.

What Happens When SLAs Aren't Enforced

  • York Risk (Orange County audit): "Service level expectations were not closely tracked" and "no financial penalties for missing SLAs."
  • Sedgwick (Royal Oak MI): Service level concerns led to one-year renewal instead of multi-year — a trust signal.
  • 45% of contractors would prefer no TPA work at all (industry survey data).
  • Payment delays: 30-90 days standard, 60-180 days for disputed claims.
  • Scope shortfall: Initial TPA estimates cover only 50-65% of actual restoration cost.

Contract Terms That Protect Contractors

  • BluSky retainage: 10% withheld until project completion — standard in subcontractor agreements.
  • BluSky fines: $25/day for dirty jobsite, $1,000 for missing safety orientation. Real consequences.
  • FirstOnSite: No liability for mold, asbestos, or viral contamination. Emergency work = temporary only.
  • Contractor Connection: 5-year workmanship warranty required. Background checks mandatory for all employees.
  • ServiceChannel: Must provide insurance certificates within 7 days or face immediate termination.
