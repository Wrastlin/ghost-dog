# IICRC Standards Reference

> Standards published by the Institute of Inspection Cleaning and Restoration Certification.
> These underpin most TPA SLA requirements for water, mold, fire, and trauma restoration.

## Key Standards

### ANSI/IICRC S500 - Water Damage Restoration
- **The** foundational standard for water mitigation SLAs
- Defines water damage categories (1-3) and classes (1-4)
- Establishes response timeframes, documentation requirements, drying protocols
- CoreLogic Mitigate platform embeds S500 compliance
- **Key timeframe**: Microbial growth can begin within 24-48 hours of water intrusion
- Requires daily moisture monitoring with documented readings
- Equipment placement and monitoring must follow specific protocols

### ANSI/IICRC S520 - Mold Remediation
- Standards for professional mold remediation
- Containment, removal, and clearance testing protocols
- Documentation and post-remediation verification requirements
- Requires AMRT (Applied Microbial Remediation Technician) certification

### ANSI/IICRC S540 - Trauma & Crime Scene
- Biohazard cleanup standards
- PPE requirements and protocols
- Documentation and regulatory compliance

### ANSI/IICRC S700 - Fire & Smoke Damage Restoration
- Fire damage assessment and restoration protocols
- Requires Restoration Work Plan (RWP)
- Documentation standards for fire/smoke jobs

### ANSI/IICRC S300 - Residential Cleaning
- Professional residential cleaning standards

## Key IICRC Certifications Required by TPAs

| Certification | Required By |
|--------------|------------|
| WRT (Water Restoration Technician) | Accuserve (required on-site every visit), most TPAs |
| AMRT (Applied Microbial Remediation Tech) | Accuserve (required for mold), most TPAs |
| ASD (Applied Structural Drying) | Accuserve (preferred), many TPAs |
| FSRT (Fire & Smoke Restoration Tech) | Various TPAs |
| OCT (Odor Control Technician) | Various TPAs |

## Notes
- **IICRC S560 does not exist** as a published standard; those topics are covered within S500/S520/S540
- No dedicated NPS benchmark exists for restoration as a standalone segment
- Insurance industry average NPS: 35-36
- Recommended CSAT target for restoration: 85%+
